Part Two: Book Legal Setup & Publishing Identity Registration

Your book is your intellectual property. It is also your publishing asset. That asset should be properly identified, documented, protected, and prepared for professional use.

✓

Global ISBN Setup

Each book format normally requires its own ISBN, such as paperback, hardcover, eBook, and audiobook. The benefit of a properly managed ISBN structure is that each format is professionally identified and aligned across retailers, bookstores, libraries, distributors, and online platforms instead of relying on scattered platform-based records.

✓

Barcode Setup

A barcode is required for physical retail scanning and bookstore readiness. It is created from your ISBN and placed on the back cover of your print edition.

✓

Copyright Registration Support

Formal registration creates an official record of ownership and supports your ability to take stronger action if your content is copied, misused, republished, or disputed.

✓

LCCN Support, Where Applicable

For eligible U.S. authors and qualifying titles, a Library of Congress Control Number helps libraries catalog and classify the book, adding another layer of professional publishing credibility.

✓

Metadata Alignment

We help align key publishing details such as title, subtitle, author name, description, categories, keywords, format details, pricing details, and contributor information.

✓

Publishing Documentation Pack

We organize the important registration and publishing records connected to your book, helping you maintain a cleaner archive for future editions, reprints, bookstore submissions, marketing campaigns, or legal reference.

Please note: We are not acting as your attorney, and this is not legal advice. However, we do help manage the registration process and documentation support so your publishing setup is handled more professionally and with greater care.

Why we recommend doing this before publication

Timing matters. The best time to structure your book correctly is before it goes live.

It is much cleaner to build the right foundation before publication than to come back later and restructure the book after it is already live.

Once a book is published, changing ISBNs, restructuring formats, correcting metadata, expanding distribution, or aligning platform records can become more complicated. In some cases, the book may need to be republished or reconfigured, which can interrupt momentum and create unnecessary delays.

The cost of waiting is usually higher than doing it correctly now.

This is not about adding complexity. It is about avoiding future publishing cleanup.
